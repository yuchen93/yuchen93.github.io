Hi, I'm Yuchen!

I am an Assitant Professor of Computer Science at UCLA.

My research interest lies at the intersection of robot learning and human-robot interaction.

Recent advacnes in machine learning allow robots to be programmed post-deployment. Leveraging human guidance is crucial for such robots to adapt to various users and different downstream tasks. However, traditional machine learning methods are tethered to the expectation of large-scale near-optimal data, while teaching robots can be challenging for non-expert users, who may provide data lacking in both quality and quantity. My research aims to build algorithms, frameworks, and systems that facilitate efficient robot learning from human interactions. Specifically, I approach interactive robot learning through: 1) developing active learning algorithms for eliciting better data points to rapidly reduce uncertainty, 2) constructing models of human teaching signals when they explicitly or implicitly enables robot learning, and 3) designing intuitive interfaces for facilitating human low-effort teaching of robots. My ultimate goal is to democratize autonomation by making robots accessible to everyday users.

Prior to UCLA, I was an HAI postdoc fellow in the ILIAD lab, advised by Dorsa Sadigh at Stanford University. I received my PhD from the University of Texas at Austin in 2021, advised by Scott Niekum.
